Changes to Asylum Laws

New Asylum Law: This law consolidated earlier laws into one systematic and clear law regarding the asylum granting process. It was written after the first balkan war brought a large amount of refugees seeking asylum into switzerland.

No ID: No Asylum: This introduced a new requirement for granting asylum. The refugee has to provide identification documentation. This rule was added because many african refugees apparently threw away their documentation and claimed to be from war-ridden countries despite being from a peaceful country. Such refugees are called economic refugees because they flee not from danger for their life but instead seek a better life. Unfortunately for them, switzerland does not grant asylum to economic refugees.

Dublin Regulation: This introduced the rule that any refugees anywhere in the EU and switherland, has to ask for asylum in the first country they enter. This prevents refugees from traveling from one country to the next and asking multiple times for asylum.

No Asylum at Embassy: Before this change, it was possible to enter a swiss embassy anywhere in the world and ask for asylum. After this change, the refugee has to be physically present in switzerland in order to be able to ask for asylum.
